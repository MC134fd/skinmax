Redesigned Analytics

What each section does

  • Range picker (7D / 30D / 90D / 1Y) — Cal AI pattern. 30D is default (long enough to see trends, short enough to stay motivating).
  • Glow trend chart — the weight line for skin. Shows 30-day average prominently, delta vs prior period, today as highlighted dot, dashed green average line as a benchmark.
  • Skin Age card — the big Cal-AI-style signature metric. Composite of texture + hydration + pigmentation + firmness vs chronological age. Goes dark theme to feel premium and different.
  • VISIA-inspired surface grid — Texture, Redness, Pores, Hydration. These four are the legit metrics an iPhone camera + AI can actually estimate. Each with a score, delta arrow, progress bar.
  • Radar chart — today vs 30-day average as two overlapping polygons. Shows which metrics are above/below personal baseline at a glance.
  • Consistency heatmap — GitHub-style. Rewards showing up, not perfection. Coral intensity = days logged. "87% this month" beats "12-day streak" for long-term retention (research-backed).
  • Food → skin impact ranking — the killer feature no one else has. Shows which foods correlate with glow-ups in YOUR data. Togglable between boosters and offenders.
  • Weekly report card — narrative summary in Glowbite voice ("Thursday glowed brightest"). Best day, toughest day, trend, personal-best streak.
  • Goal tracker — single goal at a time. Personalized target ("reach Glow 85 in 30 days"). Progress bar with % complete.
  • Streak milestone — shows current + countdown to next badge, so there's always a near-term reward.
03 · Under the hood

Design notes

Honest decisions about what's real vs marketing, and what belongs on a phone screen.

Metrics we use (can estimate from camera + AI)

  • Texture — wrinkles, pore size, microrelief. Standard CV task.
  • Redness / Erythema — RGB color space analysis. Plausible with standardized selfie.
  • Pigmentation / Clarity — uneven brown/red patches.
  • Hydration (visual) — estimated from surface gloss + puffiness. We call it "Hydration Visual Index" — never claim medical accuracy.
  • Firmness (visual) — jaw definition, fine lines depth.
  • Skin Age — composite of the above vs age-graded reference dataset.

Metrics we DON'T use (need hardware)

  • TEWL (Transepidermal Water Loss) — needs an evaporimeter.
  • Corneometer hydration — needs skin contact probe.
  • Sebumeter oil measurement — needs tape strip + light reading.
  • Cutometer elasticity — needs a suction probe.
  • Including these would be dishonest and hurt trust. Better to be transparent about what the app can/can't measure.

Retention patterns I borrowed

  • Cal AI / MyFitnessPal: range picker, trend line with delta, goal card.
  • Oura: weekly narrative ("Thursday glowed brightest"), context for peaks/valleys.
  • Lose It: consistency heatmap rewarding "5 of 7 days" over perfect streaks (cuts burnout).
  • Whoop: radar/breakdown of subcomponents — but simplified, not clinical.
  • Duolingo: milestone countdown ("3 days to next badge") for always-in-sight reward.

Glowbite's unique angle

  • Food → skin impact ranking — no one else does this. Correlates foods user logged with glow-score changes 24h later.
  • Skin Age — instantly legible, shareable, sticky. The "metabolic age" for skin.
  • Self-comparison only — never "your glow vs. other women." Compares user to their own 30-day baseline.
  • Playful medical honesty — "Hydration Visual Index" not "Hydration Score." Builds trust over time.
