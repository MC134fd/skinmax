The fix

Alternating section backgrounds do the job

v5 used thin 1-pixel rules to separate the 4 features. Too subtle — users had to work to find where one ended and the next began. v6 uses four distinct background colors (blush → cream → white → cream) so sections are obvious the moment you land. This is Flo's exact trick.

Each section also gets a numbered eyebrow label in coral ("1 · THE GLOW-UP") and a big bold headline. Three signals of "new section" at once: color change, numbered tag, headline.

Structural changes from v5
  • Alternating section backgrounds — blush / cream / white / cream
  • Numbered section labels — "1 THE GLOW-UP" in coral caps
  • Headline + subhead pattern in every section, copied from Flo
  • Right-side "more →" link on every section head (Apple Health pattern)
  • Time range toggle (Today/Week/Month/Year) at top — top-right pattern from MyFitnessPal

Peachy + pitchable

Flo is the north star — here's why

Flo (70M MAU, female-first, data-dense but never clinical) nails the exact tone you described: cute but professional, warm but legit, safe for a Series A pitch deck.

Their three tricks I borrowed:

  • Rose/coral on white/cream — never bright hot pink. Always soft and warm. v6 palette stays in your existing terracotta with a softer pink-leaning accent (--coral-pink) for hero elements.
  • Clean sans-serif typography — no cursive, no serifs, no cute fonts. Data-grade fonts dressed up by warm color.
  • Emojis earn their spot — only when representing a real thing (🥑 for avocado, 💡 for insight, 🔥 for streak). Never decorative sparkles.
Scanability check

3-second rule applied

The research benchmark: users should understand what they're looking at in 3 seconds without reading. v6 passes because the hierarchy cascades cleanly:

  • 1.0s — see the background color change → "new section"
  • 1.5s — see the numbered coral eyebrow → "this is section 2"
  • 2.0s — see the headline → "it's about my trend"
  • 2.5s — see the big "78" → "I'm at 78, up from 70"
  • 3.0s — scan the 2-col metric grid → "four things improved this month"

v5 couldn't do this because nothing announced a new section. v6 announces it three ways.

Pattern origins

Which pattern came from where

FloAlternating section backgrounds (blush/cream/white) + numbered eyebrow labels + subhead under headline.
OuraMinimalist metric cards, 2-column grid, colored dot + name + delta pattern (blue/green/coral/amber).
MyFitnessPalTime range toggle at top — Today/Week/Month/Year as a 4-pill segmented control.
Apple Health"More →" link at section-header right edge for progressive disclosure without burying content.
StravaHero visual first (photo/chart), stats grid second, insight callout third. Every section in v6 follows this 3-step cascade.
CalmInsight callout boxes with soft-tinted backgrounds (the blush and lavender info cards at the bottom of sections 2 and 4).
Honest tradeoff

What v6 gives up

v5's editorial, magazine feel is quieter and more "design-lit" than v6. v6 is more conventional — it looks like a real shipping app because it follows the patterns real shipping apps use. That's deliberate.

If you pitch this to investors, v6 is safer. If you win design Twitter, v5 is stronger. For a first product launch where you need users to understand the screen instantly and for the data to feel trustworthy, v6 is the right choice.
