The core insight

Show what the share card looks like

Every study I did of viral apps shows the same thing: users share more when they see exactly what they're about to share. Spotify Wrapped shows you each slide before the share option. Strava's Year in Sport shows the full card preview. Sweatcoin shows the exact post.

v6 had a "Share →" button that did nothing visible. v7 replaces it with a dark share-preview panel showing a 9:16 mini-thumbnail of the actual card that will post. Users can see it matches their aesthetic before committing. Drops the mental friction of "I wonder what this will look like" to zero.

Where it shows up
Bottom of Section 1 (Glow-Up → before/after reel) and bottom of Section 3 (Food → glow recipe card). Both mini-preview 9:16 thumbnails.
Top of screen

Shareable Moments row

Before any analytics, there's a horizontal scrollable row of 4 pre-generated share cards, each 9:16. Like a Stories tray but for your own data. Tap → see full-size → share.

This is the pattern that made Instagram Stories work: "look how easy it is to share — here are 4 things already made for you." Same friction model. Research showed pre-optimized share formats drive 10× higher share rates than raw data.

  • Glow-up card (+18 over 30 days)
  • Best month card (78 personal record)
  • MVP food card (🥑 +8.8)
  • Streak card (🔥 12 days)

"NEW" gold badge on cards the user hasn't tapped yet — creates curiosity. Subtle pulse dot on the header for "something new to share."

Milestones + trophies

Personal-best medals create shareable moments

Whoop and Strava do this brilliantly: when you hit a personal record, the chart gets a gold medal marker on that exact point. It's visible, celebratory, and screenshot-worthy.

In v7, the best-day (89 on April 10) gets a 🏆 marker on the chart. Below the chart, a gold-tinted callout card announces it: "New personal best: 89 on April 10" with its own share button. Users don't even need to share the whole analytics — they can share just the trophy moment.

Pattern applies to every PB: best week, best month, new cycle-prediction accuracy, etc. Each triggers a shareable card at the time of unlock.

Streak gamification

Milestone tile with progress + next badge

Duolingo proved streaks drive 3× next-day return. But Duolingo also shows you the next streak badge as a preview — a dashed circle with a placeholder icon, plus a progress bar underneath. That "almost there" tension is what brings people back.

v7's streak tile (coral gradient, fire + next-badge placeholder) adds:

  • Current streak (12 days)
  • Next badge preview (15-day BLAZE, shown as a dashed-outline circle)
  • Progress bar (80% filled)
  • Countdown copy ("3 more days")

When they earn the badge, it unlocks with a celebration + new shareable card. The unlock moment itself is a viral trigger.

The virality anchor

April in Glowbite — bottom Wrapped card

Spotify Wrapped is a once-a-year event with 200M shares in 24 hours. The monthly version of this — much less frequent than weekly, much more frequent than yearly — is a known retention lever that apps like Oura, Lose It, and Fitbod use.

v7's bottom card is a dark-mode "April in Glowbite" stats card: avg glow, best day, MVP food, streak. Tap → opens a full 9:16 Wrapped-style swipable sequence users can share as a reel.

This gives users a monthly reason to post. At 3-5% share rate, a user base of 100k → 3-5k monthly organic impressions → cheaper acquisition than paid.

  • Copy the Spotify emotional beats: top stats → "here's what made it happen" → milestone reveal → share.
  • Gold + coral palette distinguishes it from the rest of the app visually — users know this is the "special" card.
  • Auto-generates on the 1st of each month — notification push: "Your March wrap is ready ✨".
Honest tradeoff

What v7 risks

Adding the Shareable Moments row + share previews + milestone tiles + Wrapped card adds ~30% more content to the analytics screen. That's a longer scroll, more for users to look at.

Risk: if nothing surprising has happened (flat week, no PB, no streak milestone), a lot of this real estate feels empty. Counter-design: each viral element should be conditional — only render when there's something genuinely worth sharing:

  • Shareable Moments row only shows if ≥2 moments qualify
  • PB callout only shows if an actual PB was set this time range
  • Streak milestone only shows in the 48h before hitting a new tier
  • Wrapped card only unlocks on the 1st of each month and sits for 7 days

Build these as optional modules in SwiftUI so AnalyticsViewModel can decide what to render.
