## What makes an app acquirable — the 5 levers that move the multiple

Every buyer, from Flippa indie to L'Oréal BD team, scores you on these. Get them right and multiples go from 2× to 5×+.

### 1. Retention (this is the #1 factor at every tier)
- Day 30 retention > 25% for subscription apps = premium
- Day 90 retention > 15% = blue-chip
- Cal AI's secret weapon was daily habit formation (3 scans/day). Your food scans do the same. Your skin scans do NOT (weekly at most) — so the food scan has to be what you measure retention on.
- **Action: instrument D7/D30/D90 retention from day 1. Put the graph on your pitch deck.**

### 2. Revenue predictability & MRR trajectory
- Buyers pay up for "up and to the right" MRR curves, even at small absolute numbers.
- $50K MRR growing 30%/month > $200K MRR flat.
- **Action: weekly sub @ $4.99 is the right price. But also offer annual ($59.99) to smooth churn and show "longer commitment" LTV. Buyers love annual cohorts.**

### 3. Organic % of acquisition
- 100% organic traffic commands 4–5× revenue. 100% paid traffic commands 1.5–2×.
- Cal AI's TikTok organic is why it was worth so much. If you're paying for every install, you have no moat.
- **Action: log UTM source for every install. Target >50% organic from TikTok. Never share total ad spend unless you have to.**

### 4. Data moat (this is what gets you out of "app flip" and into "strategic acquisition")
Acquirers want data a competitor can't reproduce in <3 years. For you, this is:
- Proprietary **paired dataset** of food images + same-user skin images over time. This does not exist anywhere. If you have 10,000 users logging food + face scans together for 6 months, you have something Neutrogena and L'Oréal physically cannot buy.
- **Action: structure your Supabase schema to preserve food-scan → skin-delta causal pairs. Anonymize but keep. This is the crown jewel.**

### 5. Brand & cultural resonance
- "Culturally resonant" is the exact phrase Capstone uses for why some apps sell at 6× and others at 2×.
- For you: the more women 18–35 the app is identified with, the more L'Oréal/Estée Lauder will pay. This is the one area where your beautiful UI, the Nunito/coral design, and the bestie voice actually *are* financially load-bearing — they signal "we own a Gen Z female audience" which is worth a premium to a strategic buyer.

## What to build RIGHT NOW to be acquirable (product decisions)

These are specific decisions you should make in the codebase this week, ordered by impact on exit multiple:

**1. Instrument retention cohorts and expose them in a dashboard.**
Log every install with source, every subscription start/cancel, every scan. Build a simple admin page that shows D1/D7/D30/D90 retention by install cohort. This is the first slide every acquirer asks for.

**2. Ship the food-to-skin causal correlation feature.**
This is your data moat made visible. An insight card that says *"People who ate seed oils 3+ days last week saw avg –6 glow points"* is what separates you from every other skin scanner and every other food scanner. It's also what you'll show L'Oréal's BD team in month 12.

**3. Build an annual plan option.**
$4.99/week as the default. $59.99/year as a "save 77%" option. Annual cohorts show buyers you have LTV, not just churn.

**4. Add a referral or share mechanic tied to your score reveal.**
Every score reveal screen should have a 1-tap "share my glow score" option. This makes organic acquisition visible and trackable — which is what buyers price.

**5. Don't build social features, communities, forums, or coach chat.**
These feel great to users but destroy exit multiples because they create support burden, moderation risk, and unclear scope. Cal AI famously kept scope tight — that's why MyFitnessPal could integrate it cleanly. Be the *feature* a bigger platform wants to bolt on, not a *platform* competing with them.

**6. Avoid HIPAA / medical claims like a plague.**
The moment you claim "this diagnoses skin conditions" or "this is medical advice," your buyer universe shrinks by 80% due to regulatory risk. Keep copy aspirational and never prescriptive: "glow score" not "diagnosis," "inflammation risk" not "dermatitis risk."

**Option B — Grow to strategic:** Push to $100K+ MRR. Retain a boutique M&A advisor (Focus Advisors, Capstone, Raymond James consumer) at the $1M ARR mark. They'll shop you to the 10–15 real strategic buyers. 6 months of process.

### Months 13–18: close
If Option B: M&A process runs ~4–6 months from first buyer meeting to close. Expect an LOI at ~5× revenue, deal negotiations down to ~3–4×, standard 1–3 year earnout tied to MRR retention.

## The honest risk section

**1. Viral apps often can't get sold.** Cal AI was special because retention was durable — 8M+ DAU habit loop. Many "viral" apps spike to $1M MRR and are unsellable because buyers see the decline coming. Your food scan habit is your insurance against this. Your skin scan alone is not.

**2. The multiple compression is real.** Revenue multiples for consumer apps dropped from 8× (2022) to 2.5× (H2 2024). That's not coming back. Price your expectations accordingly.

**3. Big strategic buyers move slowly.** L'Oréal's BD cycle is 9–18 months. If you're burning cash, you can't wait them out. Have 12+ months of runway at exit-start or take a faster, smaller deal.

**4. Your unique dataset doesn't exist until you have ~5K active users scanning food+face for 30+ days.** Until then, your "data moat" pitch is vaporware. Month 9–12 is when the dataset becomes real.

**5. Blake Anderson's playbook ≠ guaranteed outcomes.** He's built three apps in this model. Thousands of copycats tried and failed. The part he doesn't say is that his distribution muscle (24/7 TikTok content, hundreds of accounts, friends-of-friends reposting) is a full-time job and most solo builders don't run it at that intensity. Budget for a content partner — probably $3–10K/month — or plan to do it yourself full-time for 6 months.
