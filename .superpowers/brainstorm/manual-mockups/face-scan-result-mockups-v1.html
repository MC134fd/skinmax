How it handles 8 metrics

Pro

  • Most informational — every issue is explicitly annotated.
  • Premium, editorial feel.
  • Great for "show receipts" in screenshots.

Con

  • Dense on small screens — 8 pills + lines is a lot.
  • Easy to feel clinical if copy drifts.
  • Connector lines harder to maintain as scans vary.

How it handles 8 metrics

Each metric is a floating pill anchored to its blemish zone via a dashed coral line. Pills are TagPill-style with a severity dot. Tap pill → opens SkinDetailView. Glow Score sits top-right as a compact ScoreRing.

How it handles 8 metrics

All 8 metrics shown as vertical MetricRows under the hero. Each row has emoji, short voiceover line, and a severity pill. Tap row → opens SkinDetailView. Apple-labeled insight card at the bottom replaces the generic AI insight.

Shared foundations

All three concepts share the same blemish-mapping, palette, and reusable primitives. Only the layout / framing changes.

Blemish mapping

AcneRed/pink bumps on cheeks, density scales with severity.
Dark spotsSoft brown patches near temple / cheekbone.
RednessPink blush flush across both cheeks.
HydrationMatte veil + faint dry lines (glossy shine + sparkle when good).
TextureSubtle stippling across cheek/nose zones.
ElasticitySoft sag shadow at apple bottom.
PoresTiny dot cluster on nose zone.
WrinklesThin fading lines at forehead + eye crease.

What we reuse

  • ScoreRing — used for Glow badge (A), top-right ring (B), inline ring (C).
  • TagPill — metric pills in Concept B, severity pills in C.
  • InsightCard — "Apple says" card in C.
  • HapticManager — .selection on metric tap, .success on result reveal.
  • SkinDetailView — tapping any metric (in any concept) opens the existing sheet.
  • GlowbiteColors.trafficLight(for:) — drives pill + ring color per score.
  • Typography: .gbDisplayL for Glow, .gbTitleM for section heads, .gbCaption for metric labels.
